Shohin Aheleroff

Digital Twin · Industry 4.0/5.0 · Mass Personalisation · Smart Manufacturing

Short bio

Shohin Aheleroff works in the areas of Digital Twin, Industry 4.0/5.0, mass personalisation, and smart manufacturing. His research has been published in journals and conferences such as Advanced Engineering Informatics and CIRP CMS, and has attracted more than 1,800 citations across Google Scholar and other indexing services.

His work has been recognised through several academic and innovation awards, including placement in the Top 0.5% Global Scholars (ScholarGPS 2024), the University of Auckland Distinguished Graduate Award, and the New Zealand Prime Minister’s Scholarship to Latin America. He has also received recognition for innovation and teaching, including First Prize at Auckland’s IoT & Augmented Reality Hackathon, Best Paper at the 49th International Conference on Computer & Industrial Engineering, and the New Zealand Certificate in Adult & Tertiary Teaching with High Distinction (A+).
